How South East Asia transport hub is affected by Pandemic

By: Karel Mackenzie Chandra - 30373867

In this visualisation we are going to visualise the trends in international travels-arrivals and cargo export-import on Changi airport throughout the pandemic.

Here we see how Singapore as the transport hub of South East Asia handles the new normal of Pandemic Travels, Cargo Transport, current active international routes from Singapore Airline. Lastly we are going to see the visualiation of active fleets, comparing it pre covid and covid total fleets.

Routes offered by Singapore Airlines

Throughout the Pandemic people tends to not travel more as before due to restrictions, presented from each country for incoming travles. Hence by that the Air transport industry has been hit hard in particular as people does not travel as much as before.

Departure rate in comparison to Arrival rate from the selected Countries

The travel rate of people in different countries can gives an insight on how the recovery economy rate of the country, in corresponding to the travel rate of people, not only that but certain travel restrictions to here have important role.

Fig.2

Here we can identify that arrivals and departure to China and Indonesia have the highest rate, against other country. Why is that so because the travel restrictions are less to the two countries and is not that significant in compare to other countries. Not only that but other factors such as vaccination rate and herd immunity may have been reached by those country that alreay start to rise from the crisis.

Departure Trend in Singapore from 2015-2020

Fig.3

We can see that there is a positive trend in departure up until 2020 where there is a huge drop due to major travel restrictions.

In 2019 Changi Airport handles 68.3 million passengers,

an increase in 4% from the year before, the highest it has been for 38 years.

Not only that but it also handles 2.01 million cargo in 2019.

Cargo in bound to Singapore from 2000-2020

Fig.4

Even though international travels are affected due to Pandemic we can see that the cargo passing through is still relatively high.

Seeing it from 2000, the amount of cargo passing through has gone up almost 250%. Hence airlines see an opportunity in this, they maximize the use of their fleet to carry cargo.

We can see that Containerized cargo fills in the majority of cargo coming in bound into Singapore.

Singapore Airline Active Fleet comparison

Based on articles found, we can see that Singapore airline total fleet has decrease by half of it's size. Some when through early retirement and others are stored in Alice Springs storage located in Australia, an optimal place to store these jumbo jets.

Here we can see that Singapore Airlines have mainly relied on two types of Plane which is the Boeing 777-300ER and the Airbus A350-900. However in 2020 they mainly use Airbus A350-900 as it is more cost efficient to operate on as the engine can go further then the 777-300ER.
